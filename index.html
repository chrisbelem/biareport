Key Change: A More Focused Methodology

This year, we refined our definition of a "Critical Process". This change ensures we focus our most intensive continuity efforts (like OCPs) on risks that are both severe and time-sensitive. This is why results may differ from last year's analysis.

Previous Approach

The criteria for classifying a process as critical were broader and less specific about recovery time.

Current Approach

A process is only "Critical" if it has a **High or Very High** impact with a recovery time objective (RTO) of **12 hours or less.**
